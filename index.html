Research Focus

My research centers on leveraging advanced language models and multimodal machine learning techniques to analyze longitudinal experiential data. Specifically, I work on developing large language models to predict behavioral and emotional engagement, science identity, and confidence levels over time. By integrating temporal patterns and multimodal inputs, my work aims to improve the accuracy and interpretability of AI-driven forecasting models in educational and human-centered contexts.

Additionally, I investigate robust methods for handling missing data, domain adaptation, and cross-distribution evaluation to ensure that models remain reliable across diverse populations and changing conditions. Ultimately, my goal is to build AI systems that provide actionable insights and support personalized learning and development.

Human-First AI Lab (HAL 2.0)

I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Human-First Artificial Intelligence Lab (HAL 2.0), led by Dr. M.R. Hasan, where we are dedicated to pioneering AI technologies that prioritize human values, ethical integrity, and societal benefit. Drawing inspiration from the iconic 1968 film 2001: A Space Odyssey, our lab’s name pays homage to HAL 9000—the film’s AI that serves as a cautionary tale of misalignment—while HAL 2.0 represents a new vision: developing AI that seamlessly aligns with human goals and enhances real-world impact. From transforming education and healthcare to fostering inclusive, trustworthy innovations, our research is shaping AI as a force for social good.
